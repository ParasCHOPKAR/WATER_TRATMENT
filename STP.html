Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR)

The MBBR process uses specially designed biofilm carriers suspended in wastewater. Microorganisms grow on these carriers, breaking down organic pollutants efficiently. It requires less space and ensures stable treatment performance even under variable loads.

  • ✔ Compact & cost-effective – Requires less space than conventional treatment systems, reducing civil and operational costs.
  • ✔ Handles shock loads efficiently – Maintains treatment performance even with sudden increases in influent flow or pollutant concentration.
  • ✔ Low sludge generation – Reduces sludge handling and disposal costs due to efficient biofilm growth and organic matter degradation.
  • ✔ Minimal operator attention required – Automated or semi-automated operation lowers manpower requirements.
  • ✔ Easy retrofit & scalability – Can be added to existing plants without major modifications and scaled for increasing capacity.
  • ✔ Stable treatment performance – Ensures consistent effluent quality under varying temperature and load conditions.
  • ✔ High pollutant removal efficiency – Effectively removes BOD, COD, and nutrient load, ensuring regulatory compliance.
  • ✔ Energy-efficient operation – Optimized aeration and carrier movement reduce power consumption.
  • ✔ Resistant to toxic shocks – Biofilm carriers protect microorganisms, allowing system resilience against toxic influents.

Membrane Bio Reactor (MBR)

MBR combines biological treatment with membrane filtration. It produces crystal-clear, pathogen-free water suitable for reuse in gardening, cooling, or industrial purposes.

  • ✔ High-quality effluent (almost drinking standard) – Produces clear, pathogen-free water suitable for industrial reuse, irrigation, and cooling, helping conserve freshwater resources.
  • ✔ Compact footprint – Requires less space than conventional systems, making it ideal for urban or constrained sites.
  • ✔ Eliminates need for secondary clarification – Membrane filtration replaces sedimentation tanks, reducing civil work, maintenance, and sludge production.
  • ✔ Ideal for water reuse applications – Treated water meets high standards for non-potable or industrial reuse, supporting sustainable water management.
  • ✔ Consistent effluent quality – Membrane barriers ensure reliable performance under fluctuating loads and varying influent quality.
  • ✔ Reduced sludge production – Efficient microbial retention minimizes sludge generation and associated disposal costs.
  • ✔ Easy integration & automation – Can be integrated with remote monitoring, SCADA systems, and automated cleaning cycles.
  • ✔ Energy-efficient operation – Optimized aeration and membrane systems reduce operational energy requirements.
  • ✔ Minimal chemical usage – Reduces reliance on coagulants or flocculants, lowering operational costs and chemical handling risks.
  • ✔ Environmentally compliant – Meets stringent discharge norms, ensuring regulatory adherence and sustainability.

Sequential Batch Reactor (SBR)

The SBR is a fill-and-draw activated sludge system. Treatment occurs in batches through stages: fill, aeration, settling, decanting, and sludge removal. It’s highly flexible and efficient in nutrient removal.

  • ✔ Flexible operation for varying loads – Can handle fluctuations in influent flow and pollutant concentration efficiently, making it ideal for industries and municipal applications with variable wastewater.
  • ✔ Excellent nutrient removal – Efficiently removes nitrogen and phosphorus, reducing eutrophication risks in receiving water bodies and ensuring compliance with environmental standards.
  • ✔ Suitable for medium to large installations – Modular design allows scaling up for larger capacities without compromising treatment efficiency or quality.
  • ✔ Automated cycle operation – Sequential fill, react, settle, decant, and idle stages can be fully automated, minimizing operator intervention and optimizing performance.
  • ✔ High-quality effluent – Produces treated water suitable for discharge or reuse in non-potable applications like irrigation, cooling, or landscaping.
  • ✔ Reduced footprint – Compact design compared to continuous-flow systems, saving space in urban or industrial sites.
  • ✔ Low sludge production – Batch operation and controlled aeration reduce excess sludge, lowering disposal costs and operational complexity.
  • ✔ Energy-efficient – Aeration and pump cycles are optimized to reduce electricity consumption while maintaining treatment efficiency.
  • ✔ Easy monitoring and control – Compatible with SCADA systems and remote monitoring for real-time performance tracking and predictive maintenance.
  • ✔ Regulatory compliance – Consistently meets discharge standards for BOD, COD, TSS, and nutrients, ensuring adherence to CPCB/SPCB norms.

Rotating Biological Contactor (RBC)

RBC uses large rotating discs partially submerged in wastewater. As the discs rotate, microorganisms attached to the surface degrade organic matter effectively.

  • ✔ Low energy consumption – Uses passive rotation and natural aeration, significantly reducing electricity costs compared to conventional aeration systems.
  • ✔ Simple & reliable operation – Mechanical simplicity ensures minimal breakdowns and easy operation, suitable for small to medium-scale installations.
  • ✔ Low sludge generation – Efficient biofilm-based treatment reduces excess sludge production, lowering disposal costs and operational efforts.
  • ✔ Long service life with minimal maintenance – Durable construction and slow-moving components result in extended equipment lifespan with limited maintenance requirements.
  • ✔ Effective organic load removal – Continuously removes BOD and COD from wastewater, ensuring stable effluent quality.
  • ✔ Resilient to load variations – Can tolerate fluctuations in flow and pollutant concentration without affecting treatment efficiency.
  • ✔ Compact design – Requires less footprint compared to conventional activated sludge systems, ideal for space-constrained sites.
  • ✔ Environmentally friendly – Low noise, low energy, and reduced chemical usage make it sustainable and eco-friendly.
  • ✔ Easy monitoring and automation – Compatible with sensors and SCADA systems for real-time performance monitoring and control.
  • ✔ Compliance with discharge standards – Efficiently meets regulatory norms for industrial and municipal wastewater treatment.

Activated Sludge Process

The Activated Sludge Process (ASP) is a widely used biological wastewater treatment method that utilizes a dense population of microorganisms to decompose organic pollutants. Dissolved oxygen is continuously supplied through aeration to support microbial growth and metabolism. This process efficiently converts biodegradable waste into carbon dioxide, water, and stabilized sludge, resulting in high-quality effluent suitable for discharge or reuse.

Key advantages include:

  • ✔ Effective removal of BOD and COD from municipal and industrial wastewater.
  • ✔ Flexible system design suitable for small, medium, and large-scale treatment plants.
  • ✔ Can be integrated with nutrient removal processes for nitrogen and phosphorus reduction.
  • ✔ Supports process automation and remote monitoring for enhanced operational control.
  • ✔ Produces stabilized sludge that can be further treated or safely disposed of.
  • ✔ Resilient to fluctuating inflow and pollutant load, ensuring stable effluent quality.
  • ✔ Energy-efficient aeration options available to reduce operational costs.
  • ✔ Compliant with environmental regulations for safe discharge into water bodies.

Electro Coagulation System

The Electro Coagulation (EC) System is an advanced water and wastewater treatment technology that employs an electric current to remove suspended, emulsified, and dissolved contaminants. Through electrolysis, charged ions destabilize pollutants, forming flocs for easy separation. Effective in treating heavy metals, oil & grease, dyes, and color for industrial, municipal, and commercial applications.

Key advantages and features include:

  • ✔ Effective removal of heavy metals such as Pb, Cd, Cr, Hg, and As.
  • ✔ Reduces turbidity, color, and COD in wastewater.
  • ✔ Minimizes use of chemical coagulants, making it environmentally friendly.
  • ✔ Generates compact sludge that is easy to dewater and dispose of.
  • ✔ Capable of handling varying wastewater volumes and fluctuating pollutant loads.
  • ✔ Compact system design suitable for retrofitting in existing plants.
  • ✔ Energy-efficient operation with low maintenance requirements.
  • ✔ Compliant with environmental discharge standards for safe release or reuse.
  • ✔ Can be integrated with other treatment technologies such as filtration, MBR, or SBR for enhanced performance.
