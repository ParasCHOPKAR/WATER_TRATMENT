Activated Sludge Process

The Activated Sludge Process is a biological wastewater treatment method that uses a mass of microorganisms to break down organic pollutants. Oxygen is supplied to support microbial growth, which converts impurities into carbon dioxide, water, and stable sludge.

Electro Coagulation System

The Electro Coagulation System is an advanced water and wastewater treatment process that uses electric current to remove contaminants. It destabilizes suspended, emulsified, or dissolved particles, enabling them to aggregate and be easily separated. Effective in removing heavy metals, oil & grease, and color.
