Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF) Unit

Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF) is an advanced water treatment process that efficiently clarifies wastewater by removing oil, grease, suspended solids, and organic contaminants. It is widely used in industries where high levels of suspended and floating particles are present.

The DAF system works by dissolving pressurized air into water and then releasing it into a flotation tank. Tiny air bubbles attach to suspended solids and oil droplets, lifting them to the surface to form a floating layer called sludge. This sludge is then skimmed off using mechanical or automated scrapers, leaving clarified water suitable for further treatment or discharge.
